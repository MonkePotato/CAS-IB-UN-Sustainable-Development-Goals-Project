The Journey Towards Sustainable Development

How humanity moved from solving immediate problems to building a sustainable future.

A Timeline of Global Sustainability

1987

Brundtland Report

The concept of sustainable development gained global recognition through the report "Our Common Future", defining sustainability as meeting present needs without compromising future generations.

2000

Millennium Development Goals (MDGs)

The United Nations introduced eight goals focused on reducing extreme poverty, improving education, and increasing global health.

2012

Rio+20 Conference

Countries began developing a broader framework that included environmental, economic, and social sustainability.

2015

The Sustainable Development Goals Are Created

193 United Nations Member States adopted the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development containing 17 goals and 169 targets.

2030

Target Year

The deadline for achieving the SDGs and creating a more sustainable world.

Why Were These 17 Goals Chosen?

The SDGs were designed because global challenges cannot be solved independently. Poverty affects education. Education affects employment. Employment affects inequality. Climate change affects food, water, health, and migration. The 17 goals represent a connected system where progress in one area can accelerate progress in others.

🌎 Universal

Unlike previous development goals, the SDGs apply to every country, including wealthy nations.

⚖ Balanced

They combine three dimensions of sustainability:
Social development
Economic growth
Environmental protection

🔗 Connected

The goals recognise that solving one problem often requires solving many others simultaneously.

IB Student Perspective

For IB students, the SDGs provide a framework for turning learning into action.

  • CAS: Create projects supporting local communities or environmental causes.
  • ESS: Investigate sustainability issues through scientific analysis.
  • TOK: Explore questions such as "Who decides what progress means?"
  • Extended Essay: Research real-world sustainability challenges.
